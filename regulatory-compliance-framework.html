Executive briefing · Regulated industries

The regulatory compliance framework.

A seven-part view of the meeting-edge governance gap across financial services, healthcare, and public-sector organizations.

01 / Executive view

AI meeting tools create a new control surface.

When employees invite personal or unapproved notetakers, sensitive audio, transcripts, and summaries can move beyond the organization’s approved technology and governance boundaries.

Financial servicesHealthcareGovernment
02 / The problem

The exposure is operational before it is regulatory.

The immediate problem is loss of control: an unknown service can capture a regulated conversation without the organization’s normal review, retention, monitoring, or vendor-management workflow.

Unknown data path

Audio and transcripts may be processed or retained in third-party environments.

Incomplete oversight

Security and compliance teams may not know the bot joined or what it captured.

Unmanaged records

Meeting outputs can sit outside approved retention, legal hold, and supervision processes.

Vendor uncertainty

Terms, safeguards, and downstream data use may not have completed formal review.

03 / Coverage map

The same bot can intersect with multiple frameworks.

Which requirements matter depends on the organization, meeting, participants, data, jurisdiction, and intended use of the resulting record.

04 / Financial services

Supervision and recordkeeping begin in the meeting.

01
Customer and investor data

Meeting capture can involve personal information, account context, recommendations, or confidential strategy.

02
Books, records, and retention

Unapproved transcripts may not follow the firm’s approved archive, immutability, or legal-hold process.

03
Supervisory evidence

Firms need a defensible way to show which tools were permitted and how unauthorized use was handled.

05 / Public sector

Authorization and accountability travel together.

Approved cloud use

Agencies need clarity about which services are authorized for the data and mission involved.

Privacy review

Meeting tools may process personal or sensitive information that belongs inside formal assessment workflows.

Records management

Outputs may become agency records and should not live only in an employee-controlled account.

Auditability

Governance teams need evidence of attempted access, policy decisions, and enforcement actions.

06 / Healthcare

Clinical context raises the stakes.

Care discussions can combine protected health information, clinician judgment, billing context, consent requirements, and safety-critical decisions. A personal notetaker can create a data flow outside the approved clinical environment.
