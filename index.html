Coverage and limitations

Know what each platform can show.

Platform coverage varies. NoteTakerGuard shows what each connected platform can observe, what it cannot verify, and where customer action is required.

Zoom

Connection
Customer-authorized account connection and approved access scope.
Visibility
Participant and meeting activity that Zoom exposes to the connected environment.
Response
Available actions depend on host permissions, account settings, and Zoom capabilities.
Known gap
Listening-only or externally controlled tools may require customer review.

Microsoft Teams

Connection
Tenant administrator approval and customer-selected permissions.
Visibility
Participant and meeting events Microsoft makes available to the connected tenant.
Response
Available actions vary with organizer rights, tenant policy, and Microsoft capabilities.
Known gap
Some automated activity may not appear as a conventional meeting participant.

Google Meet

Connection
Google Workspace administrator approval and an agreed evaluation scope.
Visibility
Participant and meeting signals Google exposes to the connected workspace.
Response
Available actions depend on host role, workspace policy, and Google capabilities.
Known gap
Tools operating outside exposed participant signals may need manual follow-up.

Evaluation FAQ

What buyers need to know.

Clear answers about data, platform permissions, response options, and what happens after the Shadow Audit.

Does NoteTakerGuard record the meeting?

No. NoteTakerGuard is designed to observe meeting-governance signals and automated participant activity; it does not record the meeting itself.

What does the 48-hour Shadow Audit capture?

Within the approved scope, the audit observes platform-exposed meeting and participant events, policy signals, platform responses, and actions taken. The resulting report supports internal review.

How are permissions and retention handled?

The customer approves the platform connection, meeting scope, and permissions used for the evaluation. Data handling and retention expectations are confirmed during onboarding; NoteTakerGuard does not record the meeting itself.

What happens when the 48 hours end?

Monitoring access pauses, while the report remains available. The customer chooses a paid pilot, technical review, expanded evaluation, or close-out.

Are Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Google Meet identical?

No. Coverage and response options vary by platform, account configuration, host role, permissions, and the signals each provider makes available.

Does NoteTakerGuard certify compliance?

No. It supports meeting-governance, security, privacy, and internal review workflows. It does not determine legal obligations or replace counsel, compliance officers, or required regulatory controls.
